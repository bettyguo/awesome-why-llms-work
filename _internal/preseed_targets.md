## What we will and will not do

### Will

- Mention public accounts naturally in launch posts where their work is cited.
  Tagging a researcher whose paper we annotated is fine when the post is about
  that paper.
- Submit short pitches to newsletters with a 100-word pitch + link.
- Cross-post to forums in text-post form, on-topic, with substantive openings.

### Will not

- DM any researcher who has not publicly engaged with this repo first.
- Send mass-tagged Twitter/X posts that are not relevant to each tagged person.
- Email newsletter operators a generic "please cover this" message; we send
  topic-specific, substantive briefs.
- Buy stars. Solicit stars in unrelated communities. Run a "growth hack" of any
  kind that would reduce our credibility.

## Pre-launch trust list

A subset of the above are individuals or labs we might invite to *pre-read*
the repo before public launch (T-7 days, per the launch playbook). The criterion
is: would their feedback meaningfully improve the repo? It is not "who has the
biggest follower count?"

We do not publish the trust list specifically because the invitation step is
private even though the list of candidates is public. The actual invitation is
sent via whatever the candidate's preferred channel is (a non-public email
address, in most cases).
