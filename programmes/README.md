## Structure of every programme file

1. **Hard Core** — the falsifiable central claim, stated as a single sentence and then unpacked.
2. **Protective Belt** — auxiliary hypotheses that absorb local refutations.
3. **Positive Heuristic** — what kind of research the programme generates.
4. **Key Papers (annotated)** — primary sources, each with the schema in [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](../CONTRIBUTING.md).
5. **Surveys and Reviews** — separate from primary sources.
6. **Falsification Status** — list of specific claims with statuses.
7. **Open Problems** — 3–7 concrete questions a PhD student could pick up.
8. **Reading Path (Beginner → Researcher)** — pedagogical gradient.
9. **Mini-Experiment** — pointer to the corresponding notebook.

## Status taxonomy (used across all programmes)

- 🟢 **Supported** — at least two independent replications, or the original paper plus an accepted survey, with no published refutation.
- 🟡 **Contested** — at least one named credentialed critic with a cited counter-paper.
- 🔴 **Refuted** — a cited refuting paper accepted by the community (workshop or above).
- ⚪ **Open** — a serious claim with no published replications or refutations yet.

Status changes are PRs of their own; see [`.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/status-change.md`](../.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/status-change.md).
